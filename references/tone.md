# Workshop Tone and Audience

## Audience

- Curious adults with varied backgrounds
- No assumption of prior expertise
- Assume they're smart and can follow complex ideas
- Some attend out of casual interest, others with deep expertise

## Voice

- Engaging but not performative
- Informative but not pedantic
- Conversational and approachable
- Humble — sharing, not lecturing

## What to Avoid

- Talking down to the audience
- Condescending framings: "Beginners welcome", "Let's start with the basics", "Most of us know far less...", "The most misunderstood..."
- Audience labels or special treatment language
- Fake enthusiasm or hype
- Overly technical jargon without explanation

## Titles and Labels

- Plain descriptive titles only — "The History of Coffee" is enough
- No dramatic subtitles, no compound try-hard titles
- Bad: "From Humble Bean to Global Phenomenon", "Everything You Didn't Know About Coffee"
- Good: "Who Grew the First Coffee Plants?", "Coffee Routes: Trade and Economics"
