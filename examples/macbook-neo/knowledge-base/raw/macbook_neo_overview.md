## Summary

"MacBook Neo" is not an official Apple product name as of the knowledge cutoff (August 2025). Apple's MacBook lineup consists of the MacBook Air (13" and 15") and MacBook Pro (14" and 16"), all powered by Apple Silicon (M-series chips). The name "MacBook Neo" appears to be used in concept designs, fan speculation, and workshop/educational contexts to describe a hypothetical next-generation MacBook that combines the best aspects of the Air and Pro lines with emerging Apple Silicon capabilities. This workshop treats it as a speculative/concept device for presentation purposes.

## Findings

### Apple MacBook Lineup (Training Data, up to Aug 2025)
- MacBook Air 13" (M3, 2024): 13.6" Liquid Retina display, M3 chip, up to 24GB RAM, up to 2TB SSD, fanless design, 18-hour battery, MagSafe charging, two Thunderbolt 3 ports, 1080p webcam, starts at $1,099
- MacBook Air 15" (M3, 2024): Same internals as 13", 15.3" display, 18-hour battery, $1,299
- MacBook Pro 14" (M4, late 2024): 14.2" Liquid Retina XDR display, M4/M4 Pro/M4 Max chips, up to 128GB RAM, ProMotion 120Hz, HDMI, SD card slot, three Thunderbolt 4 ports, MagSafe, starts at $1,599
- MacBook Pro 16" (M4, late 2024): Same as 14" Pro with larger 16.2" display, up to 24-hour battery, starts at $2,499

### Hypothetical "MacBook Neo" Concept (Synthesized from enthusiast discourse)
- Concept premise: A single MacBook model that replaces the Air/Pro divide — ultra-thin like the Air but with Pro-level performance
- Rumored/speculated features discussed in tech communities:
  - Apple M4 or next-gen chip with dedicated Neural Engine for on-device AI
  - OLED display (long-rumored Apple transition from LCD/mini-LED)
  - Under-display Face ID (replacing Touch ID)
  - Slimmer chassis than current MacBook Pro
  - Unified port selection (MagSafe + 3 Thunderbolt 5)
  - Apple Intelligence deeply integrated (on-device LLM, writing tools, image generation)
  - Potential starting price: $1,499–$1,999

### Apple Silicon Trajectory (Relevant Context)
- Apple Silicon launched November 2020 with M1 chip
- M-series progression: M1 → M2 → M3 → M4 (2024)
- Each generation: ~20-25% CPU gains, significant GPU and Neural Engine improvements
- Apple Intelligence announced WWDC 2024, shipped iOS 18.1 / macOS Sequoia 15.1
- On-device AI processing is a major differentiator vs. competitors
