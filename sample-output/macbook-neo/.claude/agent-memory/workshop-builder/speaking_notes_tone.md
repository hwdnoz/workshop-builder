# Speaking Notes Tone Guide

## Voice Principles

- **First person, present tense** — write like you're saying it out loud in the moment
- **Natural over polished** — slightly imperfect is fine, robotic is not
- **Conversational** — like talking to a friend, not reading a script
- **Short sentences are good** — but don't chop the flow unnaturally
- **No formal transitions** — avoid "furthermore", "in conclusion", "it is worth noting"

## Spoken Connectors (Use Liberally)

- "So" (starts a new thought)
- "Here's the thing" (introducing an insight)
- "What's interesting is" (drawing attention to something)
- "Think about it this way" (reframing or simplifying)
- "The cool part is" (genuine enthusiasm)
- "Notice that" (pointing out a detail)
- "That leads us to" (moving to next idea)

## Examples of Good Voice

✓ "So coffee didn't actually reach Europe until the 1600s. Think about it — the whole world we know today kind of runs on this plant, but for centuries it was just in the Horn of Africa."

✓ "Here's the thing about Ethiopian coffee — the plants grow wild there, which is wild. Most of the world's coffee is planted and managed, but Ethiopia's coffee basically grows on its own in the forests."

✓ "What's interesting is how quickly everything changed once coffee hit the trade routes. Within a generation, you've got coffee shops in Vienna, Istanbul, London. It's like the social media of the 1600s."

## Examples of Bad Voice (Don't Use)

✗ "Coffee is believed to have originated in Ethiopia. Thereafter, it spread to the Arabian Peninsula..." (formal, written, distant)

✗ "It is worth noting that the cultivation of coffee expanded significantly during the colonial era." (academic, stiff)

✗ "One could argue that coffee fundamentally transformed global commerce." (overly formal, pretentious)

## Structure for Speaking Notes

Each slide section should include:
- **Opening line** (one sentence, no title restatement)
- **Key points** (2-3 bullets, conversational language)
- **Transition** (one sentence leading to next slide)
- **Tips** (pacing, emphasis, where to pause)

## Formatting

```
## Slide [N]: [Title]
**Time**: [X min] | **Opening**: [one sentence] | **Transition**: [one sentence]
- [key point, conversational]
- [key point, conversational]
**Tips**: [pacing or emphasis note]
```

## Supplementary Sections

Include at the end:
- **Pre-Talk Checklist** — room setup, tech check, water, etc.
- **Timing Guide** — cumulative minutes per section
- **Glossary** — any terms that might need clarification
- **References** — where you got your info
