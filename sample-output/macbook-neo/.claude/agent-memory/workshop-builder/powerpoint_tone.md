# PowerPoint Tone Rules

## Title Style

- Plain, descriptive titles only — "The History of Coffee" is enough
- Do not add a dramatic subtitle
- No compound titles that try to be clever or punchy: "The History of Coffee: From Ethiopia to Your Cup in 500 Years" — hard no. That style feels in-your-face and try-hard

## No Condescending Language

Never write or imply any of these framings:
- "Beginners welcome" (sounds arrogant)
- "Most of us know far less..." (implies audience is ignorant)
- "Let's start with the basics" (insulting)
- "The most misunderstood..." (patronizing)

## Voice

- Humble and informative — sharing, not lecturing
- Assume the audience is intelligent and curious
- No flattery, no audience labels, no special treatment language
- Straightforward and clear

## Examples of Good Titles

- "Who Grew the First Coffee Plants?"
- "Coffee Routes: Trade and Economics"
- "From Bean to Cup: Processing Methods"
- "Cultural Significance Across Regions"

## Examples of Bad Titles (Don't Use)

- "Who Grew the First Coffee Plants? A Fascinating Journey Through Ethiopian Highlands"
- "Coffee: The World's Most Beloved Beverage (Did You Know?)"
- "From Humble Bean to Global Phenomenon"
- "Everything You Didn't Know About Coffee"
