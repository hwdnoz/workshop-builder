# Workshop Style and Audience

## Overall Tone

- Engaging but not performative
- Informative but not pedantic
- Respectful of audience intelligence
- Conversational and approachable

## Audience Assumptions

- Listeners are curious adults with varied backgrounds
- No assumption of prior expertise or knowledge
- Some may attend out of casual interest, others deep expertise
- Assume they're smart and can follow complex ideas

## What to Avoid

- Talking down to the audience
- Treating the audience like children
- Making assumptions about what people "should" know
- Overly technical jargon without explanation
- Fake enthusiasm or hype

## Interaction Elements (Optional)

Consider including:
- **Questions for reflection** — "How do you think coffee spread so quickly?"
- **Interactive moments** — "Raise your hand if you've tried coffee from Ethiopia"
- **Polls or quizzes** — brief, fun, not graded
- **Audience discussion time** — leave space for questions

## Pacing

- Expect 5-10 minutes per major section (adjust based on audience size and interaction)
- Build in pauses for questions
- Don't rush through interesting details
- Allow time for the audience to process surprising facts

## Visuals Support the Story

- Images should be high quality and relevant
- Avoid generic stock photos
- Each visual should add meaning, not just decoration
- The presentation should work even without detailed explanation

## Customization for Your Audience

If this is being tailored for a specific group (coffee industry, history enthusiasts, etc.), note:
- What they already know (skip basics, go deeper)
- What they care about (industry impact, cultural significance, flavor, etc.)
- What questions they likely have
- What format works best (formal, casual, interactive, etc.)
