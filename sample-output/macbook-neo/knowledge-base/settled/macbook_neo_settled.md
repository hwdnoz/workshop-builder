## Settled Facts

- "MacBook Neo" is not a current official Apple product name — it is a conceptual/speculative device used as a workshop subject. — Source: Apple product catalog (Aug 2025)
- Apple's current MacBook lineup uses Apple Silicon M-series chips (M1 through M4 as of 2024). — Source: Apple.com
- The MacBook Air (M3, 2024) features a fanless design, 13.6" or 15.3" Liquid Retina display, up to 24GB RAM, 18-hour battery, MagSafe + 2x Thunderbolt 3, starts at $1,099. — Source: Apple.com
- The MacBook Pro 14" (M4, late 2024) features a 14.2" Liquid Retina XDR display with 120Hz ProMotion, M4/M4 Pro/M4 Max chips, up to 128GB RAM, HDMI, SD card slot, 3x Thunderbolt 4 + MagSafe, starts at $1,599. — Source: Apple.com
- Apple Intelligence (on-device AI) was announced at WWDC 2024 and shipped in macOS Sequoia 15.1, running on M-series chips. — Source: Apple WWDC 2024
- The "MacBook Neo" concept synthesizes: ultra-thin Air-class chassis + Pro-class performance, OLED display, under-display Face ID, Thunderbolt 5, and deep Apple Intelligence integration. — Source: Synthesized from analyst/enthusiast community
- Each M-chip generation delivers roughly 20–25% CPU performance gains over its predecessor. — Source: Apple Silicon benchmark data
- The MacBook Neo concept targets creative professionals, developers, and power users who want a single premium device without choosing between Air portability and Pro performance. — Source: Conceptual framing

## Notes

This is a workshop on a conceptual product. Presenters should acknowledge the "Neo" name is speculative and use it as a vehicle to discuss Apple Silicon trajectory, design trends, and on-device AI — all grounded in real Apple products and roadmap signals.
