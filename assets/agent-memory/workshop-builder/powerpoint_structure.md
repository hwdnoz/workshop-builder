# PowerPoint Structure

## Slide Organization

- **Slide 1**: Title slide (topic, presenter, date)
- **Slide 2 or 3**: Agenda / Table of Contents (lists all major sections)
- **Slides 4+**: Content organized by section with section breaks between each major movement
- **Final slide**: Closing / call to action

## Image Requirements

- **Image density rule**: Every section must have at least one image placeholder slide
- Image placeholders should be large — at least half the slide
- Format: Use `<rect>` + `<text>` label like `[IMAGE: Coffee processing facility]`
- Good image types for coffee history: maps showing origin regions, historical engravings, plantation photos, roasting equipment, coffee shop interiors, portraits of key figures, timeline visuals

## Text Density

- **Max 4 bullet points per slide** — if you have more, split across two slides
- **Short phrases only** — no full sentences
- Each bullet should be one line of text, max 10 words
- Avoid jargon and overly complex wording

## Slide Types

- **Title slides**: Large text, minimal content, high visual impact
- **Section break slides**: Section title + visual element, transition between major movements
- **Content slides**: Headline + 2-4 bullets + image placeholder
- **Stat/callout slides**: Large number or quote + supporting text
- **Timeline slides**: Visual representation of progression
- **Closing slide**: Summary or next steps

## Visual Hierarchy

- Consistent font sizes and spacing
- Clear distinction between headlines and body text
- Section labels visible on content slides (top-left or header area)
- Slide numbers on every slide

## Speaker Notes

- Every slide must have speaker notes in the presentation's notes field
- One short paragraph per slide
- Should explain what you'll say, not restate the slide content
